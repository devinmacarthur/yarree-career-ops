# Startup Checklist & Timeline — Yarrow's Practice Launch

**Prepared:** 2026-04-06
**Target:** Licensed Acupuncturist (LAc) practice in Portland, OR — Fall 2026

---

## Pre-Launch (Now — September 2026)

### Licensure & Credentials
- [ ] Complete acupuncture program at Oregon College of Community Acupuncture
- [ ] Schedule and pass NCCAOM certification exam
- [ ] Apply for Oregon LAc license through Oregon Medical Board (~$325)
- [ ] Verify all CE requirements are current
- [ ] Get malpractice insurance quote (OAAM or AAC, $300–$600/year — $1M/$3M if planning to credential with insurers)

### Insurance Groundwork (No-Cost Steps — Do Now Even If Not Billing Insurance Yet)
- [ ] Apply for NPI Type 1 (individual) at nppes.cms.hhs.gov (free, immediate)
- [ ] Apply for NPI Type 2 (organizational) after business entity formed (free)
- [ ] Create CAQH ProView profile at caqh.org (free — used for future credentialing)
- [ ] Create superbill template with CPT codes (97810–97814), ICD-10 codes, and NPI
- [ ] See [insurance-acceptance-guide.md](insurance-acceptance-guide.md) for full insurance strategy

### Business Foundation
- [ ] Choose business name for acupuncture practice (separate from Dog Heart PDX)
- [ ] Register business with Oregon Secretary of State (~$100)
- [ ] Get EIN from IRS (free, online, immediate)
- [ ] Open dedicated business bank account
- [ ] Get Portland business license (~$100/year)
- [ ] Set up basic bookkeeping (Wave — free, or QuickBooks Self-Employed)
- [ ] Find a CPA familiar with self-employed healthcare practitioners

### Space & Location
- [ ] Research clinic spaces in target neighborhoods (Cully, St. Johns, Lents, Foster-Powell)
- [ ] Visit 3–5 potential spaces
- [ ] Explore sublease/shared space options with existing practitioners
- [ ] Consider pop-up model as interim (like North Portland Community Acupuncture)
- [ ] Budget: $800–$1,200/month for 400–700 sq ft

### Financial Preparation
- [ ] Calculate actual monthly expenses (confirm $3,333 target)
- [ ] Begin saving for startup costs — target $5,000
- [ ] Begin saving emergency buffer — target $10,000 (3 months expenses)
- [ ] Set up system for quarterly estimated tax payments
- [ ] Reduce Crema hours if/when financially feasible

### POCA & Community
- [ ] Upgrade to POCA Provider membership ($50/year) if not already
- [ ] Access CA101 Wiki for clinic startup resources
- [ ] Visit 2–3 community acupuncture clinics as a patient (study operations, flow, vibe)
- [ ] Connect with POCA forum for startup advice
- [ ] Begin conversations with Portland queer/trans health orgs (Marie Equi Center, Q Center, Pulse Wellness Co-op)

## Launch Month (October/November 2026)

### Space Setup
- [ ] Sign lease
- [ ] Acquire recliners (6) — used, $300–$600 total
- [ ] Set up treatment room (recliners, cart, supplies, lighting, music)
- [ ] Stock initial supplies: needles, cotton, alcohol, moxa, cups ($400–$600)
- [ ] Install signage
- [ ] Set up waiting/intake area

### Systems
- [ ] Choose scheduling system (Jane App is popular for acupuncture, or Acuity)
- [ ] Create intake forms (health history, consent, sliding scale explanation)
- [ ] Set up payment processing (Square — Yarrow already familiar)
- [ ] Set sliding scale: $25–$50 per treatment
- [ ] Create simple cancellation/no-show policy

### Legal & Insurance
- [ ] Activate malpractice insurance policy
- [ ] Get general liability insurance ($500–$800/year)
- [ ] Post required licenses in clinic
- [ ] HIPAA compliance: secure patient records, privacy practices

### Marketing (Minimal)
- [ ] Create Google Business Profile (free, essential for local search)
- [ ] Build simple website (Squarespace ~$16/mo or free Carrd page)
- [ ] Register on POCA Clinic Locator (free with clinic membership)
- [ ] Print business cards / referral postcards
- [ ] Tell your communities: POCA, acupuncture school cohort, dog clients, Crema regulars, friends
- [ ] Introduce yourself (with cards) to referral partners:
  - [ ] Marie Equi Center
  - [ ] Pulse Wellness Cooperative
  - [ ] Q Center
  - [ ] PridePoint Health
  - [ ] Local naturopaths, therapists, massage therapists, midwives

### Soft Launch
- [ ] Open for appointments: 3 shifts/week, 3–4 hours each
- [ ] Keep Crema at 2–3 shifts/week
- [ ] Keep Dog Heart PDX running as normal
- [ ] Set initial goal: 10–15 patients/week

## Growth Phase (Month 2–6, Winter 2026 — Spring 2027)

### Patient Volume
- [ ] Increase to 4 shifts/week as demand grows
- [ ] Track patient volume, average payment, and retention weekly
- [ ] Ask for Google reviews from satisfied patients
- [ ] Begin building referral relationships (send thank-you notes to referring providers)

### Operations
- [ ] Review and adjust sliding scale if needed
- [ ] Refine scheduling flow based on actual patterns
- [ ] Order supplies in bulk once you know your consumption rate
- [ ] File first quarterly estimated taxes (January 2027)

### Crema Off-Ramp
- [ ] Track monthly: acupuncture net + dog income vs. $3,333 target
- [ ] **Exit trigger:** 2 consecutive months at $3,333+ net with $10K buffer saved
- [ ] Give Crema proper notice when ready
- [ ] Target: exit Crema by Spring/Summer 2027

---

## Expansion Phase (Month 7–18, Spring 2027 — Fall 2027)

### Increase Acupuncture Volume
- [ ] Move to 5 shifts/week, 4 hours each
- [ ] Target: 20–30 patients/week
- [ ] Consider adding evening or weekend shifts based on demand

### Launch Pathway 2 (Integrated Wellness)
- [ ] Start 1 yoga class/week at rented studio (The People's Yoga, Awakenings)
- [ ] Host first wellness workshop (pick one: Gua Sha for Stress, Hormone Health 101, etc.)
- [ ] Test pricing and attendance
- [ ] Announce via patient base + referral partners

### Business Development
- [ ] Evaluate LLC conversion (consult CPA — benefits kick in at ~$40K net/year)
- [ ] Evaluate insurance credentialing — if patient demand warrants, begin applications with Regence and Providence (3–6 month process). See [insurance-acceptance-guide.md](insurance-acceptance-guide.md)
- [ ] Explore OHP/Health Share of Oregon credentialing if serving Medicaid patients
- [ ] Build email list for workshop/class announcements
- [ ] Formalize referral partnerships

## Established Phase (Month 18+, Fall 2027 onward)

### Full Practice
- [ ] Acupuncture: 5 shifts/week at moderate-high volume
- [ ] Yoga: 1–2 classes/week
- [ ] Workshops: 1–2/month
- [ ] Pop-ups: occasional/seasonal
- [ ] Dog Heart PDX: as desired
- [ ] Target net income: $3,500–$5,000/month

### Long-Term Considerations
- [ ] Consider nonprofit formation (501(c)(3)) for grant eligibility
- [ ] Explore health equity grants (OHA, CareOregon, Oregon Community Foundation)
- [ ] Develop branded workshop series
- [ ] Consider hiring associate acupuncturist if demand exceeds capacity
- [ ] Professional development: additional CE, specializations, conferences
