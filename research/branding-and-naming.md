## 2. Tagline Ideas

1. **"Healing in good company."** — Community acupuncture in six words. Warm, simple, true.

2. **"Sliding scale. Open doors. Real care."** — Three pillars, direct, no ambiguity about accessibility.

3. **"Your body already knows the way."** — Somatic trust, patient empowerment, trauma-informed.

4. **"Rooted care for every body."** — "Every body" does double duty (everyone + physical body). Inclusive without being clinical.

5. **"Rest is part of the treatment."** — Speaks to the group room experience, trauma recovery, counter-hustle-culture.

6. **"Community care at the speed of healing."** — Anti-rush, anti-productivity-culture, POCA values.

7. **"Where healing is not a luxury."** — Direct statement on access and equity. Slightly confrontational in a good way.

8. **"Bien soigne. Well cared for."** — Bilingual tagline, French/English. Elegant and personal.

9. **"Come as you are. Heal as you can."** — Radical acceptance, sliding scale implied, no prerequisites.

10. **"Acupuncture for the rest of us."** — Populist, funny, demystifying. Strong POCA energy.

## 3. Brand Positioning Statement

Yarrow is a trauma-informed community acupuncturist in Portland, Oregon, offering sliding-scale care in a group setting where healing is accessible, unhurried, and grounded in respect for every patient's autonomy and identity. With specialized focus on hormone health, chronic pain, and trauma recovery — and deep commitment to serving queer, trans, and multilingual communities — Yarrow bridges clinical expertise with the anthropologist's understanding that health is always shaped by culture, access, and belonging. This is acupuncture that meets people where they are: financially, physically, and as whole human beings.

## 4. Visual Direction Notes

### Color Palette

| Role | Color | Hex (approx) | Rationale |
|------|-------|---------------|-----------|
| Primary | Deep moss green | #4A6741 | PNW, grounding, healing, gender-neutral |
| Secondary | Warm cream/oat | #F2E8D5 | Warmth, accessibility, softness, not sterile white |
| Accent | Muted terracotta or clay | #C47B5A | Warmth, earth, body, approachable |
| Text | Charcoal (not black) | #2C2C2C | Softer than pure black, easier to read, less clinical |
| Optional accent | Soft sage or seafoam | #A8BFA0 | Lightness, breath, calm |

### Aesthetic Vibe

- **Warm modernism.** Not boho-cluttered, not sterile-clinical. Think: a well-lit Portland coffee shop that happens to do acupuncture.
- Clean lines with organic textures (linen, wood grain, pressed botanicals).
- Photography style: natural light, real bodies, diverse patients at rest. No stock-photo wellness cliches (no white women in white rooms holding jade rollers).
- Illustration over photography where possible — line drawings of plants, needles, hands. Feels handmade and approachable.
- Typography: one clean sans-serif for body text (e.g., DM Sans, Plus Jakarta Sans) paired with one warm serif or humanist font for headings (e.g., Fraunces, Lora, or Newsreader). Avoid anything that reads as "luxury spa."

### Anti-References (What to Avoid)

- All-white minimalism (signals exclusivity)
- Yin-yang imagery or stereotypical "Oriental" aesthetics (cultural flattening)
- Pastel pink/lavender as primary (reads gendered despite good intentions)
- Overly clinical blue/white (hospital vibes)
- Cursive script logos (hard to read, signals a different demographic)

### Positive References

- POCA cooperative branding (direct, grassroots, clear)
- Portland mutual aid orgs' visual language (hand-drawn, earnest, inclusive)
- Patagonia-style nature photography (real places, real texture)
- Queer health clinics like ZAMI NOBLA, Fenway Health, or Callen-Lorde for tone

## 5. Online Presence Checklist

### Must-Have at Launch

- [ ] **Google Business Profile** — Critical for local search. Claim immediately once you have a physical address. Category: "Acupuncturist." Add sliding scale info, pronouns-friendly language, photos of space.
- [ ] **POCA Cooperative Directory** — List the practice on the POCA (People's Organization of Community Acupuncture) clinic directory at pocacoop.com. This is where community acupuncture patients actively search.
- [ ] **Simple Website** (see below)
- [ ] **Instagram** — Primary social channel for Portland wellness audiences. Post: space photos, educational content (what does community acupuncture look like?), sliding scale explainers, practitioner intro.
- [ ] **Inclusive Therapists / Therapy Den** — These directories allow filtering for LGBTQ+-affirming providers and increasingly list acupuncturists and bodyworkers.

### Website Recommendations

**Platform:** Squarespace or Carrd (simple, fast, affordable).
- Squarespace if you want booking integration, blog capability, and room to grow ($16-23/mo).
- Carrd if you just need a single-page site to start ($19/year).

**Essential Pages:**
1. **Home** — Who you are, what community acupuncture is, who it's for (everyone), sliding scale range
2. **About Yarrow** — Background, training, values, pronouns, languages spoken, photo
3. **Services & Pricing** — Community acupuncture, gua sha, cupping, moxa, yoga. Sliding scale explained clearly and without shame.
4. **Book an Appointment** — Online scheduling (Acuity/Jane App/Noterro integration). Include new patient intake form.
5. **FAQ** — "What should I wear?" "Is it a group room?" "Do I have to explain my sliding scale choice?" (Answer: No.)
6. **Contact / Location** — Address, transit access, parking, accessibility notes (wheelchair access, scent-free policy, etc.)

## 6. Name Availability Considerations

### Oregon Business Name Search

- Search the Oregon Secretary of State Business Name Database: sos.oregon.gov/business
- Check for exact matches AND confusingly similar names (the state may reject names too close to existing ones)
- You can reserve a name for 120 days before filing ($10 fee)
- Consider registering as an LLC or assumed business name (DBA) depending on your business structure
- Search early — do not fall in love with a name before checking

### Domain Availability Tips

- Check availability at Namecheap, Porkbun, or Google Domains (now Squarespace Domains)
- If .com is taken, strong alternatives for a local practice:
  - `.health` — professional, clear
  - `.clinic` — direct, clinical credibility
  - `.community` — on-brand for POCA model
  - `practicenamePDX.com` — Portland convention, often available
- Buy the domain as soon as you decide, even before building the site ($10-15/year)
- Also consider buying common misspellings if the name is unusual

### Social Media Handle Tips

- Check handle availability across platforms simultaneously at namechk.com or knowem.com
- Prioritize: Instagram > TikTok > Facebook (in that order for Portland wellness)
- If the exact handle is taken, Portland conventions that work:
  - `@practicename.pdx`
  - `@practicenamePDX`
  - `@practicename.acupuncture`
- Secure handles on platforms you plan to use ASAP, even if you don't post yet — just to hold the name
- Keep the handle consistent across all platforms for discoverability

### Cross-Check with Existing Brands

- Search USPTO trademark database (tess2.uspto.gov) for your top name choices
- Google the name + "acupuncture" + "Portland" to check for local conflicts
- Check Instagram and Facebook for any Portland-area practices using similar names
- Verify the name doesn't have unintended meanings in French or Spanish (given multilingual branding)
