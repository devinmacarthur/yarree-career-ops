## 1. Community Acupuncture Clinics (Sliding Scale / POCA Model)

These are Yarrow's closest comparables — clinics using the open-room, sliding-scale, high-volume model.

### Working Class Acupuncture (WCA)
- **Locations:** 4 clinics in Portland
  - **Hillsdale** — SW Beaverton-Hillsdale Hwy (SW Portland)
  - **Cully** — NE 42nd Ave (NE Portland)
  - **Rockwood** — SE 182nd Ave (East Portland / Gresham border)
  - **NE** — NE 42nd & Wygant (NE Portland)
- **Pricing:** Sliding scale $25-$45 per treatment. Student clinic $10-$20.
- **POCA-affiliated:** Yes — WCA founded the community acupuncture movement and co-created POCA. Also operates POCA Tech (Oregon College of Community Acupuncture).
- **Patient focus:** General community acupuncture. No specific population niche — serves everyone.
- **Notes:** The original. Founded 2002 by Lisa Rohleder and Skip Van Meter. 4 locations means broad geographic coverage but concentrated in NE/SW/East Portland. WCA is not a competitor so much as the ecosystem — their existence validates the model and their overflow/referrals benefit other community clinics.
- **Website:** https://www.workingclassacupuncture.org/

## 2. Affordable / Low-Cost Acupuncture (Non-Community Model)

These clinics offer reduced-cost acupuncture but use a private-room or hybrid model rather than the classic open-room POCA setup.

### OCOM Community Clinic (Oregon College of Oriental Medicine)
- **Location:** SE Portland (OCOM campus, near SE Division / 75th area)
- **Pricing:** Reduced rates (typically $15-$30) — student clinic supervised by licensed practitioners
- **POCA-affiliated:** No (academic institution)
- **Patient focus:** General; student teaching clinic
- **Notes:** High-quality affordable care but limited hours, academic calendar dependent. Students treat under supervision. Good option for price-sensitive patients but not a direct competitor to a professional community clinic.
- **Website:** https://ocom.edu/

### NUNM Community Clinic (National University of Natural Medicine)
- **Location:** SW Portland (NUNM campus, near SW Porter / Barbur area)
- **Pricing:** Reduced rates for student clinic visits (typically $15-$35)
- **POCA-affiliated:** No (academic institution)
- **Patient focus:** General; teaching clinic with multiple modalities (naturopathy, Chinese medicine, nutrition)
- **Notes:** Another teaching clinic. Limited hours, academic calendar. Broad integrative medicine offering.
- **Website:** https://nunm.edu/

### Multnomah County / OHSU Integrative Medicine
- **Location:** Various (OHSU campus and affiliated clinics)
- **Pricing:** Insurance-based; some sliding scale programs
- **POCA-affiliated:** No
- **Patient focus:** Hospital/medical system integrated acupuncture
- **Notes:** Institutional setting. Not community acupuncture but serves as a referral point for patients whose insurance covers acupuncture.

## 3. Private Practice Acupuncturists (Higher Price, Different Model)

Portland has a large number of private-practice acupuncturists. These are NOT direct competitors to a community model clinic (different price point, different patient population) but are part of the broader landscape.

### Notable Private Practices (Representative Sample)
These tend to cluster in inner Portland neighborhoods (Hawthorne, Alberta, Mississippi, NW 23rd, Pearl):

| Practice Type | Typical Pricing | Typical Location | Notes |
|--------------|----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Solo private practice | $80-$150/session | Inner SE, NE, NW | 1:1 treatments, 60-90 min |
| Boutique/wellness studio | $100-$175/session | Pearl, NW, Division | Often combined with massage, herbs, facials |
| Integrative health clinic | $90-$140/session | Various | Acupuncture alongside naturopathy, chiropractic |
| Specialized (fertility, sports) | $100-$200/session | Various | Niche focus, premium pricing |

**Key point:** Private practice acupuncture in Portland typically runs $80-$175 per session. Community acupuncture at $25-$50 serves a fundamentally different market segment. These are complementary, not competing.

### Geographic Gaps — Best Opportunities

3. **SE inner (Division/Hawthorne/Clinton corridor)** — High foot traffic, dense population, lots of wellness-oriented residents. OCOM's student clinic is nearby but limited. A professional community acupuncture clinic could thrive here, though rents are higher.

4. **Beaverton** — Large Latinx/Hispanic population could benefit from Yarrow's Spanish-language capacity. No community acupuncture identified. MAX Blue Line accessible. Rents comparable to outer Portland.

### Currently Well-Served Populations
- General working-class patients (WCA's core demographic)
- Students and low-income patients (WCA student clinic, teaching clinics)
- General pain and wellness patients

### Underserved Populations — Gaps Yarrow Can Fill

| Population | Current Service Level | Yarrow's Fit | Notes |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------|-------|
| **Queer & trans patients** | No acupuncture clinic explicitly centers this population | STRONG | Portland has robust queer health infrastructure (Marie Equi Center, Q Center, PridePoint) but no community acupuncturist specifically marketing to and affirming this community |
| **Hormone health (HRT support, menstrual, menopausal)** | Some private practitioners offer this at $100+/session | STRONG | Huge demand, especially among trans patients on HRT. Community pricing makes this accessible. |
| **Spanish-speaking patients** | Very limited in acupuncture specifically | STRONG | Portland's Latinx community (especially in Cully, Rockwood, Lents, Beaverton) is underserved by acupuncture. Bilingual care is rare. |
| **French-speaking patients** | Essentially zero | MODERATE | Smaller population but unique differentiator. Congolese, West African, Haitian communities in Portland. |
| **Trauma survivors** | Some trauma-informed therapists; not common in acupuncture specifically | STRONG | Yarrow's dual training (acupuncture + 200hr trauma-informed yoga) is genuinely rare |
| **BIPOC communities** | WCA serves diverse areas but doesn't specifically center BIPOC identity | MODERATE | Yarrow's anthropology background and cultural responsiveness training is an asset |
| **Chronic pain patients seeking affordable alternatives** | WCA serves this but has waitlists at some locations | STRONG | More community clinics = more access |

### Niche Positioning Summary

**No Portland community acupuncture clinic currently offers:**
- Explicitly queer/trans-affirming care
- Spanish-language acupuncture at sliding scale prices
- Trauma-informed community acupuncture (as a stated specialty, not just a general practice)
- Hormone health specialization at community pricing
- French-language acupuncture of any kind

This is a genuinely open niche. Yarrow is not competing with WCA — they are serving populations WCA does not specifically reach.

## 7. Market Observations

### Is the Market Saturated?

**No.** Here is why:

1. **Community acupuncture is effectively a WCA monopoly.** Four WCA locations dominate, with a couple of smaller/intermittent clinics. There is room for additional community clinics, especially in underserved neighborhoods and niches.

2. **Private practice acupuncture is saturated in inner Portland** — there are many practitioners in the $80-$150 range clustered in trendy neighborhoods. But this is a different market from community acupuncture.

3. **Demand exceeds supply.** WCA locations have reported high patient volumes and waitlists at peak times. Portland's progressive, health-conscious population is receptive to acupuncture. The limiting factor is access (geographic, financial, cultural), not demand.

4. **Population growth in outer Portland** (Lents, Cully, Rockwood, St. Johns) is creating new demand in areas with limited wellness infrastructure.

### Market Trends

- **Community acupuncture is growing nationally.** POCA reports 175+ member clinics. The model is proven and expanding.
- **Oregon insurance coverage for acupuncture** (ORS 743A.078 mandate + OHP coverage) supports the broader market. Note: OHP transitioning away from the Prioritized List in 2027 — monitor for coverage changes.
- **Post-pandemic demand for affordable healthcare** has increased interest in sliding-scale models across all modalities.
- **Queer/trans healthcare is a growth area** in Portland, with new clinics and organizations launching regularly. Acupuncture is an underrepresented modality in this space.
- **Trauma-informed care** is increasingly expected by patients, not just a nice-to-have. Practitioners who can credibly claim this (Yarrow's 200hr yoga + anthropology + clinical experience) have an edge.

### The Vibe

Portland's acupuncture scene is collegial, not cutthroat. The POCA cooperative ethos emphasizes abundance over scarcity — more community clinics serving more people is the goal, not market share protection. WCA actively supports new community acupuncture startups through POCA resources. Yarrow entering the market with a differentiated niche and POCA membership should be welcomed, not resisted.

## 9. Recommended Next Steps

1. **Verify clinic status.** Use POCA's clinic locator (pocacoop.com/clinics) and Google Maps to confirm which clinics are currently operating, their exact pricing, and any new entrants since early 2025.

2. **Visit 2-3 community acupuncture clinics as a patient.** Experience WCA at 1-2 locations plus any other community clinics still operating. Observe: scheduling flow, room setup, intake process, patient demographics, neighborhood vibe.

3. **Choose a neighborhood.** Based on gap analysis, the strongest options are:
   - **St. Johns** — community-minded, affordable rent, transit-accessible, underserved by community acupuncture
   - **Lents / Foster-Powell** — very affordable, growing, diverse, MAX access, no community acupuncture
   - **Inner SE** (if budget allows) — high foot traffic, wellness-oriented population, proximity to referral partners

4. **Connect with referral partners early.** Introduce yourself to Marie Equi Center, Pulse Wellness Co-op, PridePoint, and Q Center before opening. These relationships are your marketing engine.

5. **Talk to POCA.** As a member, Yarrow has access to the CA101 wiki, financial templates, and the collective's startup support. Use it. Ask about Portland-specific market intel — POCA members know things that are not on the internet.
