## 1. UX Researcher (Entry/Junior Level)

### Salary Range (Portland)
| Level | Annual Salary |
|-------|--------------|
| Entry/Junior UX Researcher | $65,000-$85,000 |
| Mid-level (2-3 yrs) | $85,000-$110,000 |
| Senior (4+ yrs) | $110,000-$145,000 |
| Remote roles (national companies) | $70,000-$100,000 entry |

### Remote Options
Excellent. UX research went heavily remote during/after COVID. Many roles are fully remote or hybrid. Portland has a strong tech scene (Intel, Nike, Columbia Sportswear, Puppet, New Relic, Zapproved) but remote roles from SF/Seattle companies are also accessible.

### Competitiveness: STRETCH (but achievable with portfolio work)
Yarrow's qualitative research methods, ethnographic interviewing, thesis field study design, and anthropology degree are *exactly* what UX research values. However, the gap is:
- No UX-specific portfolio or case studies
- No familiarity (demonstrated) with UX tools (UserTesting, Optimal Workshop, Dovetail, Figma)
- No tech industry experience

### What Yarrow Needs to Apply
1. **UX Research portfolio** -- 2-3 case studies. Can reframe thesis work as a UX case study (research plan, recruitment, methodology, synthesis, insights, recommendations). Can also do 1-2 volunteer/spec projects.
2. **UX bootcamp or certificate** (optional but helpful) -- Google UX Design Certificate (Coursera, ~$240, self-paced), or UXPA courses. NOT required but signals commitment.
3. **Learn the tools** -- practice with Optimal Workshop, Maze, or similar. Free tiers available.
4. **Reframe resume** -- emphasize: research design, participant recruitment, qualitative analysis, stakeholder presentations, cross-cultural research.
5. **Network** -- Portland has active UX meetups (Portland UX, Ladies that UX PDX, UXPA Oregon).

### Example Portland Employers
- Nike (Beaverton) -- large UX research team
- Intel (Hillsboro) -- UX research roles
- Columbia Sportswear (Portland)
- Cambia Health Solutions (Portland)
- Puppet / Hyland (Portland)
- Zapproved (Portland)
- Remote: UserTesting, Spotify, Mozilla, Salesforce, many startups

### Benefits
Full tech benefits: health/dental/vision, 401k with match (often 4-6%), PTO (15-20 days), stock options at some companies, professional development budget.

### Side Acupuncture Compatible?
Yes, especially if remote/hybrid. Standard 40hr weeks with flexibility. Could do evening/weekend acupuncture practice.

### Income Jump vs. Crema
**+$25,000 to +$50,000/year** (roughly doubling income) at entry level.

**Note:** Multnomah County and Oregon Health Authority use published pay scales. County positions typically start at Step 1 of the assigned range.

### Remote Options
Mixed. OHA has gone more hybrid/remote post-COVID. County positions are often hybrid (2-3 days in office). Hospital/health system roles are mostly on-site.

### What Yarrow Needs to Apply
- For CHW: Oregon CHW certification (or willingness to obtain -- employer sometimes pays)
- For Health Educator: CHES certification preferred (not always required)
- For Program Evaluator: emphasize research design, data collection, qualitative analysis, report writing
- All: highlight community engagement, cultural competency, language skills

### Example Portland Employers
- **Multnomah County Health Department** -- large employer, union positions, good benefits
- **Oregon Health Authority (OHA)** -- state agency, Salem-based but many Portland positions
- **Kaiser Permanente** -- Community Health roles
- **Providence Health** -- community health, health equity roles
- **OHSU** -- community health research positions
- **CareOregon** -- Medicaid managed care, community health
- **Health Share of Oregon** -- CCO, community health
- **Virginia Garcia Memorial Health Center** -- FQHC, Spanish skills very valuable

### Benefits
Excellent at government/health system employers. County: PERS retirement (employer-funded pension), health/dental/vision (very low employee cost), 12+ holidays, union protections, step increases. Health systems: similar quality, 403b match, tuition assistance.

### Side Acupuncture Compatible?
Yes, particularly at county/state (predictable schedules, no on-call). Health systems may have less flexible schedules.

### Income Jump vs. Crema
**+$8,000 to +$30,000/year** depending on role, plus dramatically better benefits (pension, health insurance, PTO).

Increasingly hybrid. State of Oregon allows many positions to be remote or hybrid (office 1-2 days/week). City of Portland is more in-office. Multnomah County is mixed.

### Competitiveness: MODERATE to STRONG
Government values graduate degrees. Yarrow's MS, grant experience, research skills, languages, and community engagement background are competitive. The key challenge is navigating government application systems (long, detailed applications with supplemental questions).

### What Yarrow Needs to Apply
- **Master the application format:** Government apps require detailed responses to supplemental questions. Treat each question like a mini-essay demonstrating relevant experience.
- Emphasize: data analysis, program evaluation, community engagement, equity & inclusion, cross-cultural competency
- Languages are a significant asset for community-facing roles
- Be prepared for slow timelines (8-16 weeks from application to start)

Very compatible. Predictable 8-5 M-F schedule. Many positions allow compressed schedules (e.g., every other Friday off on 9/80). Union protections for schedule consistency.

## 8. Translation / Interpretation

### Salary Range (Portland)
| Role | Annual Salary |
|------|--------------|
| Staff Interpreter (healthcare/court) | $45,000-$60,000 |
| Staff Translator | $42,000-$58,000 |
| Freelance Interpreter | $25-$55/hr (variable) |
| Freelance Translator | $0.10-$0.25/word |
| Conference Interpreter | $400-$800/day |
| Translation Project Manager | $50,000-$65,000 |

### Remote Options
Strong for translation (written work can be done anywhere). Interpretation increasingly offers remote/video options (VRI - Video Remote Interpreting) but healthcare/court interpreting is often on-site.

### Competitiveness: MODERATE
French is a smaller market in Portland than Spanish. Spanish is high-demand but also high-supply. Yarrow's combination (French native + Spanish intermediate + English fluent) is relatively rare and valuable. However:
- Professional interpretation requires certification (Oregon does not require state certification for most settings, but healthcare facilities prefer nationally certified interpreters)
- Court interpreting requires Oregon Judicial Department certification
- Medical interpreting: CCHI or NBCMI certification preferred

### What Yarrow Needs to Apply
- **For healthcare interpreting:** Complete a 40-hour medical interpreter training (Bridging the Gap or similar, ~$500-$1,000), then pursue CCHI or NBCMI certification
- **For court interpreting:** Pass the Oregon Court Interpreter certification exam
- **For freelance translation:** Build a portfolio, register with agencies, consider ATA certification
- **For staff positions:** apply to agencies or healthcare systems directly
- Spanish should be at advanced-intermediate minimum for professional interpretation

### Example Portland Employers
- **IRCO** -- interpretation/translation services division
- **Passport to Languages** (Portland agency)
- **Pacific Interpreters** (Portland)
- **OHSU** -- in-house interpreter services
- **Providence** -- interpreter services
- **Multnomah County** -- interpreter positions
- **Freelance:** TransPerfect, Lionbridge, RWS (remote)
- **Alliance Francaise de Portland** -- French-specific community, networking

### Benefits
Staff positions: standard employer benefits. Freelance: no benefits, but high flexibility. Healthcare/court interpreter staff roles typically include health insurance, PTO, retirement.

### Side Acupuncture Compatible?
Excellent, especially freelance. Interpreting is often shift-based or per-assignment, making it ideal for combining with another practice. Could potentially continue interpreting part-time even after acupuncture licensure.

### Income Jump vs. Crema
**Staff:** +$5,000 to +$20,000/year with benefits.
**Freelance full-time:** Variable, potentially +$10,000-$25,000/year but without benefits.
**Best as supplementary income** alongside another primary role.

## Strategic Notes

### Salary Negotiation
- For any role, Yarrow should negotiate. The MS degree, trilingual capacity, and federal grant experience are above-average qualifications for entry-level positions.
- Government roles have less negotiation room (step-based) but sometimes allow starting at Step 2-3 with documented relevant experience.

### Benefits Value
- Moving from Crema (likely no benefits) to a salaried position with benefits adds an estimated **$10,000-$20,000/year in total compensation** beyond the salary number (health insurance + retirement + PTO + holidays).
- This means even a $45,000 nonprofit coordinator role is effectively **$55,000-$65,000 in total compensation** vs. an estimated $34,000-$42,000 at Crema.

### PSLF (Public Service Loan Forgiveness)
- If Yarrow has federal student loans, any government or 501(c)(3) nonprofit role qualifies for PSLF after 120 qualifying payments (10 years). This could be worth tens of thousands of dollars.

### Timeline to Acupuncture Licensure
- If licensure is Fall 2026 (~6 months away), Yarrow should look for roles that:
  - Allow a predictable schedule (evenings/weekends free for side practice)
  - Don't have a 1-year commitment requirement (or be transparent about plans)
  - Alternatively: could become a long-term "day job" alongside part-time acupuncture practice, which is a very common model for new acupuncturists building a client base
