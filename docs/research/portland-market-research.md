## 1. Oregon Acupuncture Licensing

**Licensing body:** Oregon Medical Board (OMB)

**Requirements for LAc license:**
- Graduate from an ACAHM-accredited acupuncture program
- Current NCCAOM certification in acupuncture
- Maximum 4 attempts to pass NCCAOM exams — failure after 4 = ineligible for Oregon licensure
- English proficiency (satisfied by passing NCCAOM exam in English)
- Apply through Oregon Medical Board

**Renewal:** Every 2 years, 15 CE hours per year of practice required

**Key links:**
- Oregon Medical Board — https://www.oregon.gov/omb/licensing/pages/acupuncturist.aspx
- Application requirements — https://www.oregon.gov/omb/licensing/Pages/AC-Application-Requirements-and-Instructions.aspx

## 2. Insurance Paneling in Oregon

### Oregon Health Plan (OHP / Medicaid)
- OHP covers acupuncture for conditions on the Prioritized List of Health Services (Lines 1–469 as of 1/1/2025)
- PCP referral not required but recommended; should include qualifying ICD-10 diagnosis codes
- **Important change:** Starting 1/1/2027, Oregon switches from the Prioritized List to State Plan service categories — acupuncture coverage rules may change
- Coverage questions hotline: 800-336-6016
- OHP Benefits — https://www.oregon.gov/oha/hsd/ohp/pages/benefits.aspx

### Private Insurance
Oregon uses a universal credentialing application (ACPCI form, 2025 version mandatory, compliance deadline **September 14, 2026**).

**Major payers accepting acupuncture in Oregon:**
- Regence BlueCross BlueShield
- Moda / ODS
- Providence Health Plan
- PacificSource
- Aetna
- Cigna
- United Healthcare
- Kaiser
- CareOregon
- First Choice Health

### Decision for Yarree
POCA-model clinics are typically **cash-pay only** (sliding scale). Insurance paneling adds admin burden but broadens patient access, especially OHP patients. Recommend starting cash-pay, exploring OHP paneling in Year 2 if desired.

## 3. POCA Cooperative — Portland's Home Turf

Community acupuncture was invented in Portland. Working Class Acupuncture (WCA) started in 2002, founded CAN in 2006, which became POCA in 2011 — a 501(c)(6) multi-stakeholder cooperative with 175+ member clinics nationwide.

### Membership Costs
| Tier | Cost | Key Benefits |
|------|------|-------------|
| Community member | $25/year | Newsletter, birthday treatment at any POCA clinic |
| Provider member | $50/year | CA101 Wiki, financial templates, marketing plans, hiring network |
| Clinic member | $50/year | Clinic locator listing, micro-loans for startup/expansion |

### Portland-Area POCA Clinics
- **Working Class Acupuncture** — 4 locations: Hillsdale, Rockwood, Cully, NE 42nd & Wygant. Sliding scale $25–$45. Student clinic $10–$20. Also operates POCA Tech / Oregon College of Community Acupuncture.
- **North Portland Community Acupuncture** — pop-up clinic model at Turn! Turn! Turn! venue

### POCA Resources
- Main site — https://pocacoop.com/
- Membership — https://pocacoop.com/poca-membership-categories/
- WCA locations — https://www.workingclassacupuncture.org/our-clinics/clinic-locations/

## Key Takeaways

1. **$3,333/month is achievable** at ~24 treatments/week averaging $35 on sliding scale
2. **Startup costs are genuinely low** — $3,000–$6,000 for POCA-model community acupuncture
3. **Portland rent is favorable** — $500–$1,200/month for appropriate space in affordable neighborhoods
4. **Referral network is rich** — Marie Equi Center, Pulse Wellness Co-op, Q Center, PridePoint, Outside In
5. **Grant funding exists but favors nonprofits** — consider CBO partnership or eventual 501(c)(3)
6. **OHP coverage is live but limited** — 2027 system change worth monitoring
7. **Workshop venues are affordable** — $15–$50/hour, profitable with 6–8 attendees
