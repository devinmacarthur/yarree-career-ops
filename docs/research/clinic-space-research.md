## 6. Key Considerations & Lease Guidance

### Portland Commercial Lease Terms

**Typical lease structures:**
- **NNN (Triple Net):** Tenant pays base rent + property taxes + insurance + maintenance. Common in retail. NNN adds $2-5/SF to the quoted rent.
- **Modified Gross:** Landlord covers some expenses (taxes, insurance); tenant pays rent + utilities. More common in office.
- **Gross/Full Service:** All-inclusive rent. Rare in small commercial spaces.

**Lease length:** 3-5 years is standard. 1-2 year leases are possible but landlords may charge a premium. Shorter terms give Yarrow flexibility but less leverage for TI allowances.

**Negotiation leverage in 2026:**
- Portland office vacancy is at **23.1%** — landlords are motivated
- Retail vacancy is only **4.9%** — retail spaces are tighter, less negotiating room
- Office spaces converted to wellness/treatment use may offer better deals than retail storefronts
- Ask for: free rent months (1-3 months on a 3-year lease), reduced rent in year 1, TI allowance

### Tenant Improvement (TI) Allowances

- **Retail TI range:** $10-$30/SF for inline retail in strip centers
- **Office TI range:** $15-$60/SF depending on building class and lease term
- For a 1,000 SF community acupuncture space, that's potentially $10,000-$30,000 in landlord-funded improvements
- **Critical:** Get preliminary general contractor pricing BEFORE signing the lease. Actual buildout costs are often 30-50% higher than assumed.
- A community acupuncture space has minimal buildout needs: open room, basic lighting (dimmable), a few outlets, potentially a sink. This is actually a selling point for landlords — low TI cost.

### Prosper Portland ACT Grant

**Affordable Commercial Tenanting Grant** — a major resource:
- Provides funding for tenant improvements, space-related soft costs, and permitting
- Awards up to **$300,000**
- Reimbursement-based
- Eligible locations: Interstate Corridor TIF District and Gateway Regional Center TIF District
- Property must be ground floor, zoned commercial/mixed use
- Property owner must deliver space in "warm shell" standard (HVAC, drywall, flooring, ceiling, plumbing, electrical, lighting)
- Priority: businesses led by underrepresented owners, Portland-based, meeting neighborhood needs
- **Contact:** ACT@prosperportland.us
- **Application:** form.jotform.com/240456253450047

### ADA Compliance Checklist for Acupuncture Clinic

Portland and Oregon ADA requirements for a healthcare/wellness space:

**Entrance:**
- Ground floor or elevator access (ground floor strongly preferred)
- Ramp if any steps, with 1:12 slope ratio and handrails
- Door minimum 32" clear width
- Automatic door opener preferred but not required

**Interior:**
- 36" minimum aisle width throughout (between recliners)
- 60" diameter turning space for wheelchairs (at least one location in treatment room)
- At least one recliner position accessible without navigating narrow gaps

**Restroom:**
- ADA-compliant restroom (grab bars, 60" turning radius, appropriate sink height, accessible fixtures)
- If building has shared restroom, confirm it meets ADA standards

**Oregon-specific:**
- Businesses undergoing substantial remodel must spend up to **25% of remodel cost** on ADA access improvements
- New businesses and changes of ownership trigger accessibility review

**Resources:**
- NW ADA Center checklist: nwadacenter.org
- Portland ADA compliance guide: myshyft.com/blog/ada-compliance-checklist-portland-oregon/
- Bridges Oregon ADA assessment: bridgesoregon.org/ada-accessibility-assessment/

### Signage & Parking

- Portland signage regulations vary by zone. CM2 and CM3 zones allow business signage; check specific regulations for illuminated signs.
- Most Portland commercial corridors have on-street parking. No off-street parking requirement for small commercial tenants in most zones (Portland eliminated parking minimums in many areas).
- Proximity to transit stops reduces the need for dedicated parking.

### Zoning Confirmation

Acupuncture/healthcare is an **allowed use** in all Portland commercial and mixed-use zones:
- **CM1** — Small-scale neighborhood commercial
- **CM2** — Medium-scale, frequent transit corridors (explicitly allows wellness: massage, chiropractic, medical)
- **CM3** — Large-scale, high-capacity transit areas
- **CE** — Employment-focused commercial
- **CX** — Central commercial / downtown

To verify a specific address's zoning: portlandmaps.com/bps/zoning/ or contact Portland Bureau of Development Services.

## 7. Recommended Strategy

### Phase 1: Pop-Up & Relationship Building (Now through Summer 2026)

1. **Join POCA cooperative** and access forums for startup guidance and locate-a-clinic verification
2. **Contact Prosper Portland ACT program** (ACT@prosperportland.us) — get in the pipeline, even before finding a specific space
3. **Visit WCA clinics** — tour all 4 locations, meet practitioners, understand the Portland community acupuncture landscape
4. **Run 2-3 pop-up acupuncture events** at church spaces, community centers, or queer health org locations to build a patient base and community visibility
5. **Walk the corridors** in Lents/Foster-Powell and St. Johns in person — look for "For Rent" signs not listed online

### Phase 2: Secure a Space (June-August 2026)

**Priority targets (in order):**

1. **Prosper Portland ACT space in Lents Town Center** — below-market rent, grant-funded TI, ground floor, transit access, no POCA competition in the area
2. **7025 N Lombard St (630 SF)** — right size, built-out, St. Johns location (verify WCA proximity with POCA)
3. **8928 N Lombard St (900 SF option)** — modern building, food cart pod traffic, great visibility
4. **SE Foster Rd corridor** — walk and look for unlisted spaces in the $15-20/SF range
5. **Mindful Wellness Center or similar shared space** — as a backup / phased start while searching for dedicated space

### Phase 3: Buildout & Opening (August-October 2026)

- Community acupuncture buildout is minimal: dimmable lighting, recliners, a sound system, curtains/screens, reception desk
- Budget $5,000-$15,000 for buildout (less if landlord provides TI allowance or ACT grant covers it)
- ACT grant can reimburse TI and permitting costs
- Allow 4-6 weeks from lease signing to opening
