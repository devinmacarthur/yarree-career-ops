# Business Model: Integrated Wellness Practice — Portland, OR

**Prepared:** 2026-04-06
**For:** Yarrow (Yarree) — Pathway 2 (Builds on Community Acupuncture)
**Target launch:** Spring/Summer 2027 (6–12 months after acupuncture practice opens)

---

## Executive Summary

Once the community acupuncture practice is stable (Pathway 1), Yarrow layers on yoga classes, wellness workshops, and pop-up events to build a branded integrated wellness practice. This isn't a separate business — it's an expansion that increases revenue, deepens community engagement, and leverages credentials Yarrow already holds.

**Additional revenue potential:** $5,000–$15,000/year from workshops and yoga, on top of acupuncture income.

## Venue Strategy (Yoga & Workshops)

### Option A: Use Own Clinic Space (If Large Enough)
- Recliners moved aside or folded for yoga/workshop sessions
- No additional rental cost
- Requires ~600+ sq ft treatment room
- Scheduling: workshops on off-clinic days or evenings

### Option B: Rent External Space
Best-value Portland venues for regular programming:

| Venue | Rate | Best For |
|-------|------|----------|
| Awakenings Wellness Center | $15–$35/hr | Workshops (up to 50 people, 900 sq ft) |
| The People's Yoga | $30–$50/hr | Yoga classes (mission-aligned, nonprofit discounts) |
| VITALIDAD Movement Arts | Varies | Movement-focused workshops |
| Community centers | $20–$40/hr | Neighborhood workshops, accessible pricing |

### Option C: Partner Spaces
- Offer workshops at Q Center, Marie Equi Center, or partner clinics
- Often free or low-cost venue in exchange for community benefit
- Excellent for visibility and referral-building

**Recommendation:** Start with Option B or C. Move to Option A when clinic space and schedule allow.

## What Yarrow Needs to Decide

1. **Yoga classes — teach independently or at existing studios?** Teaching at The People's Yoga (established audience) vs. hosting your own (more control, more risk).

2. **Workshop frequency — how much is sustainable?** Start with 1/month and assess energy levels. Yarrow values work-life balance.

3. **Branding — unified or separate?** Recommendation: unified practice brand for acupuncture + yoga + workshops. Dog Heart PDX stays separate.

4. **Online presence — how much?** At minimum: website + Google Business Profile. Instagram if comfortable. Newsletter for workshop announcements.

5. **Nonprofit path?** If grant funding and community health mission are priorities, consider forming a 501(c)(3) in Year 2–3. Opens significant funding doors given Yarrow's profile.
