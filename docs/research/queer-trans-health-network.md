# Portland Queer & Trans Health Network Map

**Prepared:** 2026-04-07
**For:** Yarrow — Trauma-Informed Community Acupuncture Practice (Fall 2026 Launch)
**Purpose:** Map the ecosystem of queer/trans health providers, organizations, and connection points in Portland for referral partnerships, community integration, and practice visibility.

**Important note:** This document was compiled from existing knowledge as of early 2026. Yarrow should verify all details (websites, addresses, current services) before reaching out. Portland's queer health landscape is dynamic -- organizations open, close, merge, and relocate. A phone call or visit is always better than an assumption.

---

## 1. LGBTQ+ Health Clinics

These are the primary clinical organizations serving queer and trans patients in Portland. They are Yarrow's most natural referral partners -- the places where patients already go for primary care, mental health, and gender-affirming medical services.

### The Marie Equi Center (formerly Equi Institute)
- **What they do:** BIPOC-led, LGBTQIA2S+ healthcare. Primary care, behavioral health, gender-affirming care, HIV/STI services. Named after Dr. Marie Equi, a queer Portland physician and activist.
- **Location:** Brooklyn neighborhood, SE Portland
- **Website:** https://www.equiinstitute.org/
- **Population:** Low-income, unhoused, BIPOC, queer/trans patients. Trauma-informed, culturally specific care.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Very strong mission alignment -- trauma-informed, queer-centering, sliding-scale. Yarrow should introduce themselves as a community acupuncturist who shares their patient population. Propose a cross-referral relationship: Equi refers patients for pain, stress, and hormone-support acupuncture; Yarrow refers patients who need primary care, HRT, or behavioral health back to Equi. Offer to do a free pop-up acupuncture day at their clinic.

### Outside In
- **What they do:** Medical clinic, behavioral health, housing services. Strong LGBTQ-affirming care with a "Queer Zone" peer support group. Youth-focused (ages 13-24 primary population, but serves adults too). Gender-affirming care including HRT.
- **Location:** 1132 SW 13th Ave, Downtown Portland
- **Website:** https://outsidein.org/
- **Population:** Homeless and at-risk youth, LGBTQ+ youth and young adults. Integrated service model (medical + housing + employment).
- **How Yarrow connects:** Outside In often partners with complementary providers for wellness programming. Yarrow could volunteer acupuncture services for their youth program (builds goodwill and visibility). Their behavioral health team is a natural referral source for patients with trauma, anxiety, chronic pain.

### Prism Health
- **What they do:** LGBTQ-focused primary care, behavioral health, integrated pharmacy, HIV/STI services, gender-affirming care. One of Portland's most visible queer health clinics.
- **Location:** Multiple locations in the Portland metro area
- **Website:** https://www.prismhealthpdx.org/
- **Population:** LGBTQ+ community broadly, with specific programs for trans patients and people living with HIV.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Prism patients often seek complementary care for anxiety, chronic pain, and hormone-related issues -- all within Yarrow's wheelhouse. Introduce yourself with a one-page info sheet about your practice and request to be added to their community resource list. Ask about their provider fairs or community events.

### Quest Center for Integrative Health
- **What they do:** Primary care, mental health, substance use treatment, HIV/AIDS care. Over 50% of staff identify as LGBTQ+. Integrative model that already values complementary approaches.
- **Location:** NE Portland (122nd Ave area)
- **Website:** https://www.questcenterforintegrative.com/ (verify current URL)
- **Population:** LGBTQ+ patients, people living with HIV/AIDS, patients with substance use disorders, low-income populations.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Quest's integrative philosophy makes them an especially strong match. They already understand the value of acupuncture alongside conventional medicine. NADA (National Acupuncture Detoxification Association) ear acupuncture for their substance use patients could be a specific partnership angle. Ask about becoming an in-network referral partner.

### PridePoint Health
- **What they do:** LGBTQ+ primary care, HRT, PrEP/PEP, STI testing. Direct primary care model with telemedicine available in Oregon and Washington.
- **Location:** Portland (verify current office location; may operate primarily via telemedicine)
- **Website:** https://www.pridepointhealth.com/ (verify)
- **Population:** LGBTQ+ adults seeking affirming primary care, particularly for HRT and sexual health.
- **How Yarrow connects:** PridePoint patients on HRT often experience side effects (hot flashes, mood changes, pain) that acupuncture can address. Propose a referral relationship focused on hormonal health support.

## 2. Trans Health Providers & Gender-Affirming Care

These providers specifically offer HRT, gender-affirming medical care, and trans-specific health services. Yarrow's expertise in hormone health and trauma-informed care positions them well as a complementary provider.

### OHSU Transgender Health Program
- **What they do:** Comprehensive transgender healthcare -- HRT, surgical referrals, mental health, voice therapy, fertility preservation. One of the most established trans health programs in the Pacific Northwest.
- **Location:** OHSU campus, Marquam Hill + satellite clinics
- **Website:** https://www.ohsu.edu/transgender-health
- **Population:** Trans, nonbinary, and gender-diverse patients of all ages. Both adult and pediatric programs.
- **How Yarrow connects:** OHSU's program is large enough that they maintain referral lists for complementary providers. Contact their care coordination team to be listed as an affirming community acupuncturist. Their patients pre- and post-surgery, and those adjusting to HRT, are ideal candidates for acupuncture support.

### Planned Parenthood Columbia Willamette
- **What they do:** Gender-affirming hormone therapy (informed consent model), sexual health, general reproductive care. Lower barrier to HRT access than many clinical settings.
- **Location:** Multiple Portland-area locations (NE Portland, SE Portland, Beaverton, others)
- **Website:** https://www.plannedparenthood.org/planned-parenthood-columbia-willamette
- **Population:** Broad, but their gender-affirming care program specifically serves trans and nonbinary patients.
- **How Yarrow connects:** PP clinics often have community bulletin boards and resource lists. Provide flyers/cards. Their HRT patients -- especially those early in hormone therapy -- frequently seek complementary support for adjustment symptoms.

### Brave Space LLC
- **What they do:** Gender-affirming therapy, letters for gender-affirming surgery, support groups for trans and nonbinary people.
- **Location:** Portland (verify current address)
- **Population:** Trans, nonbinary, gender-questioning individuals and their families.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Therapists who work with trans clients are among the most valuable referral partners. Their patients often carry significant trauma and chronic stress -- exactly the population Yarrow is trained to serve. Send an introduction letter and offer a reciprocal referral arrangement.

### Portland-Area Informed Consent HRT Providers (various)
- Multiple individual providers and small practices offer informed-consent HRT in Portland. These shift frequently -- the most current lists are maintained by:
  - **RAD Remedy** (radremedy.org) -- trans healthcare provider directory
  - **FOLX Health** (folxhealth.com) -- telehealth HRT, maintains provider resources
  - **Trans Lifeline** resource lists
- **How Yarrow connects:** Getting listed on RAD Remedy (see Section 6) puts Yarrow directly in the search path of trans patients looking for affirming providers.

## 3. Queer/Trans Wellness Practitioners — Referral Partners

These are individual practitioners and small practices in bodywork, therapy, naturopathy, and other wellness modalities who serve queer/trans patients. They represent Yarrow's peer network -- people doing similar work in adjacent modalities.

### Pulse Wellness Cooperative
- **What they do:** Oregon's first worker-owned therapy cooperative. Counseling and psychotherapy with a queer, neurodivergent, and trauma-informed lens. Multiple therapists under one cooperative structure.
- **Location:** 1631 NE Broadway, Portland
- **Website:** https://www.pulsewellnesscoop.com/ (verify)
- **Population:** Queer, trans, neurodivergent, BIPOC clients.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Extremely strong alignment -- worker-owned cooperative model mirrors POCA values, queer-centering, trauma-informed. This should be one of Yarrow's first outreach contacts. Propose mutual referrals: Pulse refers therapy clients who would benefit from somatic/acupuncture work; Yarrow refers patients who need mental health support. Offer to co-host a workshop on body-based healing for queer/trans folks.

### Queer-Affirming Massage Therapists
Portland has a robust community of queer and trans massage therapists. Key ways to find them:
- **Portland Queer Bodyworkers** -- informal network (search Instagram, Facebook groups)
- **AMTA Oregon chapter** -- some members list LGBTQ-affirming specializations
- **Psychology Today / Massage Book** -- filter for LGBTQ-affirming in Portland
- **How Yarrow connects:** Attend Portland bodywork community events. Many massage therapists and acupuncturists develop informal referral circles. Offer to trade treatments as an introduction.

### Naturopathic Physicians (Queer-Affirming)
Oregon is one of the strongest states for naturopathic medicine. Several Portland NDs specifically serve queer/trans patients:
- **National University of Natural Medicine (NUNM)** clinic offers low-cost naturopathic care and has LGBTQ-affirming practitioners. Located on SE Division.
- Search the **Oregon Association of Naturopathic Physicians (OANP)** directory for practitioners listing LGBTQ specializations.
- **How Yarrow connects:** NDs who prescribe bioidentical hormones or support patients through gender transition are a direct referral match. Acupuncture and naturopathy are highly complementary -- many patients see both.

### Queer-Affirming Yoga Studios
- **The People's Yoga** -- Nonprofit, community-oriented, sliding scale. NE Killingsworth and SE Hawthorne locations. Already identified as a potential workshop venue (see market research). Offers queer-specific classes periodically.
- **Yoga Union** -- SE Portland. Has hosted queer-focused yoga events.
- **How Yarrow connects:** As a yoga teacher AND acupuncturist, Yarrow has a unique ability to partner with these studios. Propose a "Queer Healing" workshop series combining gentle yoga + community acupuncture. Offer to teach a class or assist with their community programming.

### Queer-Affirming Therapists and Counselors (beyond Pulse)
- **Portland Therapy Center** -- multiple therapists specializing in LGBTQ+ issues
- **Portland DBT Institute** -- some clinicians specialize in gender identity
- **Individual therapists** -- search Psychology Today filtered for Portland + LGBTQ + trauma
- **How Yarrow connects:** Therapists are the single most common referral source for acupuncturists. Build relationships with 3-5 queer-affirming therapists and the referrals will flow both directions.

### Queer-Affirming Midwives and Doulas
- Portland has a visible community of queer midwives and doulas who serve LGBTQ+ families.
- **Andaluz Waterbirth Center** -- inclusive birth center, SE Portland
- Search **DoulaMatch.net** filtered for Portland + LGBTQ-affirming
- **How Yarrow connects:** Acupuncture for fertility, pregnancy, and postpartum is well-established. Queer families seeking fertility support or pregnancy care through affirming providers would value Yarrow's practice. Cross-referrals with midwives and doulas are standard.

## 4. Community Organizations

These are the nonprofits, mutual aid groups, and community centers that form the social fabric of Portland's queer/trans community. They are not clinical partners but they are where Yarrow builds visibility, trust, and community presence.

### Q Center
- **What they do:** Portland's LGBTQ+ community center. Programs span youth, elders, arts, education, health and wellness, support groups, and community events. One of the largest LGBTQ community centers in the Pacific Northwest.
- **Location:** 4115 N Mississippi Ave, Portland (verify -- they have moved before)
- **Website:** https://www.pdxqcenter.org/
- **How Yarrow connects:** Q Center is a hub. They host wellness fairs, workshops, support groups, and community events. Yarrow should: (1) Attend events as a community member first. (2) Volunteer. (3) Propose a community acupuncture pop-up or workshop. (4) Ask to be listed in their wellness resource directory. (5) Post flyers on their community board.

### Basic Rights Oregon
- **What they do:** Oregon's largest LGBTQ+ advocacy organization. Policy, legal, and community organizing. Maintains a healthcare resource directory.
- **Location:** Portland (office location varies)
- **Website:** https://www.basicrights.org/
- **How Yarrow connects:** Get listed in their healthcare resources. Attend their community events. Follow their policy work on trans healthcare access -- good to be informed and visible.

### Black & Beyond the Binary Collective
- **What they do:** Community and advocacy for Black-African TQNI+ (trans, queer, nonbinary, intersex+) Oregonians. Mutual aid, community events, advocacy.
- **Website:** https://www.blackandbeyondthebinary.org/ (verify)
- **How Yarrow connects:** If Yarrow's practice serves this population (and it should), building a genuine relationship over time is key. Do not cold-pitch services. Attend their public events, donate, volunteer, and let the relationship develop organically.

### SMYRC (Sexual & Gender Minority Youth Resource Center)
- **What they do:** Drop-in center, support groups, housing services, health education for LGBTQ+ youth ages 13-23. A program of New Avenues for Youth.
- **Location:** Downtown Portland
- **Website:** https://www.newavenues.org/smyrc/
- **How Yarrow connects:** Offer volunteer acupuncture sessions for youth. NADA ear acupuncture is particularly well-suited for drop-in settings -- no intake required, can be done in chairs, calming effect. This is community service, not a business development play, but it builds deep trust and visibility.

### TransActive Gender Project / Gender Creative Kids
- **What they do:** Support for transgender and gender-diverse children, youth, and families. (Note: verify current status -- this organization has undergone changes.)
- **How Yarrow connects:** Awareness and support. Yarrow's practice will primarily serve adults, but knowing the full ecosystem matters for referrals.

### Portland Queer/Trans Mutual Aid Networks
Portland has robust mutual aid networks that shift and evolve. Key ones to be aware of:
- **PDX Trans Housing Fund** -- mutual aid for trans housing needs
- **Rose City T-Fund** -- financial support for trans healthcare costs
- **Portland Mutual Aid networks** (various, search Instagram and Facebook)
- **How Yarrow connects:** Mutual aid networks are trust-based. Yarrow could offer sliding-scale-to-free treatments specifically for mutual aid recipients, and let the networks know. This is a powerful way to demonstrate commitment to the community.

### TendWell
- **What they do:** Nonprofit supporting LGBTQIA+ health and wellness. Maintains resource lists and community programming.
- **Website:** https://www.tendwellnonprofit.org/
- **How Yarrow connects:** Get listed on their LGBTQIA resource page. Participate in their programming.

### Oregon LGBTQ Health
- **What they do:** Resource hub for LGBTQ health information and provider directories in Oregon.
- **Website:** https://www.oregonlgbtqhealth.org/ (verify)
- **How Yarrow connects:** Get listed as an affirming provider.

### Coalition of Community Health Clinics
- **What they do:** Network of community health centers in the Portland metro area. Maintains LGBTQ resource lists.
- **Website:** https://coalitionclinics.org/
- **How Yarrow connects:** Their resource page (coalitionclinics.org/resources/lgbtq-resources) is a referral source. Request inclusion.

## 5. Events & Gatherings

These are recurring events where Yarrow can show up, build visibility, offer services, and connect with both patients and referral partners.

### Portland Pride (June)
- **What:** Annual Pride festival and parade. Typically includes a health fair / wellness village with booths for health organizations.
- **When:** Usually mid-to-late June
- **Website:** https://www.portlandpride.org/ (verify)
- **How Yarrow connects:** Apply for a booth at the Pride health fair. Offer free ear acupuncture (NADA protocol) at the booth -- it is quick, requires minimal setup, and gives people a direct experience of Yarrow's work. This is one of the highest-visibility opportunities of the year.

### Portland Trans Pride / Trans March (June)
- **What:** Separate from the main Pride event, trans-specific celebration and march.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Attend, support, be visible. If there is a resource fair component, get a table.

### Q Center Events (ongoing)
- Q Center hosts regular programming -- health fairs, support groups, community gatherings, educational events. Check their calendar monthly.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Offer to lead a workshop on acupuncture for stress relief, or do a demo/pop-up during a wellness event.

### Portland Zine Fest / Queer-Specific Zine Events
- Portland's zine culture intersects heavily with the queer community. Yarrow could create a small zine about acupuncture, trauma, and queer health -- a great conversation starter and marketing tool that fits Portland's DIY culture.

### Queer Wellness Fairs and Pop-Ups (various)
- Portland's queer community regularly organizes wellness pop-ups, craft fairs with wellness vendors, and community health events. These are often organized through Instagram and Facebook.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Follow Portland queer event accounts on Instagram. Apply to participate as a wellness vendor offering mini-treatments or acupressure demos.

### POCA Fest / Community Acupuncture Gatherings
- **What:** POCA's annual gathering of community acupuncturists. Networking, workshops, business development.
- **Website:** https://pocacoop.com/
- **How Yarrow connects:** Attend as a POCA member. Present on the intersection of queer health and community acupuncture -- this is a genuinely novel angle that the POCA community would value.

### Oregon Acupuncture Association of Oregon (OAAOM) Events
- **What:** Professional association for Oregon acupuncturists. CE events, networking, legislative advocacy.
- **How Yarrow connects:** Join, attend events, connect with other practitioners. Good for professional network beyond the queer health ecosystem specifically.

## 6. Online Directories — Where Queer/Trans Patients Search

These are the specific directories and platforms where queer and trans patients in Portland look for affirming providers. Yarrow should be listed on as many as possible before or at launch.

### RAD Remedy (radremedy.org)
- **What:** Community-driven directory of trans-affirming healthcare providers. Users review and rate providers. High trust within the trans community.
- **Cost:** Free to be listed.
- **Priority:** HIGH. This is where trans patients look first for affirming providers.
- **Action:** Create a provider profile emphasizing: community acupuncture, sliding scale, trauma-informed, queer/trans-centering, hormone health support.

### GLMA Provider Directory (glma.org)
- **What:** GLMA (formerly Gay & Lesbian Medical Association) maintains a provider directory of LGBTQ-affirming healthcare providers.
- **Cost:** Membership-based. Individual provider membership is typically $100-200/year.
- **Priority:** MEDIUM. More used by patients seeking MDs/DOs, but acupuncturists can list.
- **Action:** Join GLMA, create a provider listing.

### Psychology Today / Therapy Directory (filtered for LGBTQ+)
- **What:** While primarily for therapists, many patients search here broadly for affirming providers. Some acupuncturists and bodyworkers are listed.
- **Priority:** LOW for Yarrow specifically (not a therapy practice), but worth knowing that patients who find their therapist here may ask that therapist for acupuncture referrals.

### FOLX Health (folxhealth.com)
- **What:** Telehealth platform for LGBTQ+ healthcare, primarily HRT. Maintains community resources and provider information.
- **Priority:** LOW for direct listing, but FOLX patients in Portland who want in-person complementary care are a potential patient source.

### Inclusive Therapists (inclusivetherapists.com)
- **What:** Directory of therapists who are affirming across multiple identities. Not directly applicable to acupuncture, but a model for how patients search.
- **Priority:** AWARENESS. Understand the search patterns.

### POCA Clinic Locator (pocacoop.com)
- **What:** Directory of POCA-affiliated community acupuncture clinics.
- **Cost:** Free with POCA clinic membership ($50/year).
- **Priority:** HIGH. Anyone searching specifically for community acupuncture in Portland will find this.
- **Action:** Register as a POCA clinic member at launch.

### Google Business Profile
- **What:** The single most important online listing for any local healthcare provider. When someone searches "acupuncture near me" or "queer affirming acupuncture Portland," Google Business Profile determines visibility.
- **Cost:** Free.
- **Priority:** CRITICAL. Set this up before opening day.
- **Action:** Create listing with: practice name, address, hours, photos, services (list "LGBTQ-affirming" and "gender-affirming" and "sliding scale" and "community acupuncture" in the description). Ask early patients to leave reviews.

### Yelp
- **What:** Still used heavily for healthcare provider searches in Portland.
- **Cost:** Free listing.
- **Priority:** MEDIUM. Claim the listing, fill it out completely. Do not pay for Yelp ads.

### Instagram
- **What:** Portland's queer community is highly active on Instagram. Many queer-affirming practitioners build their entire referral network through IG.
- **Priority:** MEDIUM-HIGH. Even a low-effort presence (occasional posts, stories about the practice, community event shares) creates discoverability.
- **Action:** Create a practice account. Follow and engage with Portland queer health organizations and practitioners. Use hashtags like #PortlandAcupuncture #QueerHealth #TransHealthPDX #CommunityAcupuncture.

## 7. Strategic Recommendations for Yarrow

### Before Launch (Summer 2026)
1. **Create profiles** on RAD Remedy, POCA Clinic Locator, and Google Business Profile.
2. **Attend Q Center events** as a community member -- start showing up before you need anything.
3. **Send introduction letters** to Marie Equi Center, Prism Health, Pulse Wellness Cooperative, and Outside In. Keep it brief: who you are, what you do, that you share their patient population, and that you would love to build a referral relationship.
4. **Volunteer** -- offer free community acupuncture at Q Center, SMYRC, or a mutual aid event. Even one session builds trust.
5. **Join POCA** as a clinic member and get on the locator.

### At Launch (Fall 2026)
6. **Portland Pride** (if timing works) -- get a booth, offer ear acupuncture.
7. **Print referral cards** -- simple cards that referral partners can hand to patients. Include: name, what community acupuncture is, sliding scale range, location, phone/website.
8. **Host an open house** -- invite referral partners and community members to see the space, experience a treatment, and learn about community acupuncture.

### Ongoing (Year 1)
9. **Build a referral circle** of 5-10 practitioners: 2-3 therapists, 1-2 massage therapists, 1-2 naturopaths, 1-2 primary care providers from the clinics above. Meet quarterly for coffee or a meal.
10. **Offer pop-up acupuncture** at community events 1-2 times per quarter.
11. **Co-host workshops** with Pulse Wellness Cooperative, The People's Yoga, or Q Center. Topics: "Acupuncture for Stress and Anxiety," "Body-Based Healing for Trans Folks," "Community Care as Healthcare."
12. **Get listed everywhere** -- every directory, every resource page, every community board.

### The Core Principle
Portland's queer health ecosystem runs on trust and relationships, not marketing. Yarrow's strongest asset is not their flyer -- it is their presence. Show up. Be genuine. Offer something before asking for anything. The referrals will follow.
