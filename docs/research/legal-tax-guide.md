# Legal & Tax Quick Guide — Yarrow's Acupuncture Practice

**Prepared:** 2026-04-07
**Situation:** Opening a community acupuncture practice in Portland, OR (Fall 2026) while also running Dog Heart PDX (dog training/pet sitting). Part-time cafe employee.
**Disclaimer:** This is a research guide, not legal or tax advice. Verify all figures with a CPA and/or attorney before acting. Rates and thresholds may change; links to official sources are provided for confirmation.

## 1. Business Structure — LLC vs. Sole Proprietorship vs. PLLC

### Option A: Sole Proprietorship (DBA)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Cost** | $50 assumed business name filing with Oregon Secretary of State |
| **Liability protection** | None. Personal assets are exposed. |
| **Tax treatment** | Pass-through on Schedule C. No separate entity tax return. |
| **Best for** | Very small, low-risk side ventures |

**Not recommended for an acupuncture practice.** Acupuncture involves bodily contact and needles — liability exposure is real even with malpractice insurance.

### Option B: Single-Member LLC (Recommended)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Filing fee** | $100 online with Oregon Secretary of State |
| **Annual report** | $100/year (due on anniversary of formation) |
| **Liability protection** | Strong. Personal assets shielded from business debts/claims. |
| **Tax treatment** | Disregarded entity by default — reported on Schedule C, same as sole prop. Can elect S-corp taxation later if income justifies it. |
| **Formation time** | Online filing at sos.oregon.gov/business — typically processed in 2-3 business days |
| **Best for** | Most solo healthcare practitioners |

**This is the standard recommendation for a solo community acupuncture practice.**

### Option C: Professional LLC (PLLC)

Oregon does NOT require a separate PLLC for acupuncturists. Oregon's professional corporation/PLLC statutes (ORS 58) apply to specific professions (physicians, attorneys, architects, etc.). Licensed acupuncturists can form a standard LLC. A PLLC would not provide additional liability protection for malpractice — professional malpractice liability still passes through to the individual practitioner regardless of entity type.

**Verdict: Skip the PLLC. A standard single-member LLC is the right move.**

### How to File Your Oregon LLC

1. Go to [Oregon Secretary of State Business Registry](https://sos.oregon.gov/business/Pages/register.aspx)
2. Click "Online Filings" and select "Domestic LLC"
3. You will need:
   - Business name (check availability first via the business name search)
   - Registered agent (can be yourself at a physical Oregon address — no PO boxes)
   - Principal place of business address
   - Organizer name
   - Mailing address
4. Pay $100 filing fee (credit card)
5. Receive confirmation typically within 2-3 business days
6. After formation, get your **EIN** at [irs.gov/ein](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online) — free, instant online

**Timeline recommendation:** File the LLC in July/August 2026, 2-3 months before opening. This gives time to open a bank account, get the business license, and set up bookkeeping before revenue starts.

## 3. Self-Employment Taxes

### Federal Self-Employment Tax

| Component | Rate | Applies To |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Social Security | 12.4% | First $168,600 of net SE income (2025 cap; adjusts annually for inflation — verify 2026 cap) |
| Medicare | 2.9% | All net SE income |
| **Total SE tax** | **15.3%** | On 92.35% of net profit |
| Additional Medicare | 0.9% | Net SE income above $200,000 (single) |

**Key detail:** You calculate SE tax on 92.35% of your net self-employment income (the IRS gives you a break equivalent to the "employer half"). You also get to deduct the employer-equivalent half (7.65%) of SE tax as an above-the-line deduction on your 1040.

### Oregon State Income Tax (2025 brackets — verify for 2026)

| Taxable Income (Single) | Rate |
|-------------------------|------|
| $0 – $4,050 | 4.75% |
| $4,050 – $10,200 | 6.75% |
| $10,200 – $125,000 | 8.75% |
| Over $125,000 | 9.9% |

Oregon has **no sales tax**, which is good — you won't collect sales tax on services. But the income tax rates are among the highest in the country. Budget accordingly.

### Quarterly Estimated Tax Payments

When you are self-employed, taxes are not withheld from your income. You must pay quarterly estimated taxes to avoid underpayment penalties.

**Federal (Form 1040-ES):**

| Quarter | Period Covered | Due Date |
|---------|---------------|----------|
| Q1 | Jan 1 – Mar 31 | April 15 |
| Q2 | Apr 1 – May 31 | June 15 |
| Q3 | Jun 1 – Aug 31 | September 15 |
| Q4 | Sep 1 – Dec 31 | January 15 (following year) |

**Oregon (Form OR-40-V):**
Same due dates as federal. Pay via [Revenue Online](https://revenueonline.dor.oregon.gov).

**When to start:** Your first quarterly payment is due for the quarter in which you first earn self-employment income from the acupuncture practice. If you open in October 2026, your first estimated payment would be due **January 15, 2027** (Q4 2026).

**Note:** Dog Heart PDX income should ALREADY be generating quarterly estimated tax obligations. If Yarrow hasn't been making quarterly payments for Dog Heart PDX, this needs to be addressed immediately — penalties and interest may apply.

**Safe harbor rule:** To avoid penalties, pay at least:
- 100% of your prior year's total tax liability, OR
- 90% of your current year's tax liability

**Practical tip:** Set aside 25-30% of every dollar of net self-employment profit into a dedicated savings account for taxes. This covers federal income tax + SE tax + Oregon income tax.

## 4. Key Deductions for an Acupuncture Practice

Every dollar of legitimate deduction reduces both income tax AND self-employment tax.

### Direct Business Expenses (Schedule C)

| Deduction | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| **Clinic rent** | Fully deductible. Likely your largest expense ($800-$1,200/month). |
| **Acupuncture supplies** | Needles, moxa, cups, linens, gowns, sanitizer, sharps containers — all deductible. |
| **Malpractice insurance** | $300-$600/year — fully deductible. |
| **General liability insurance** | Fully deductible. |
| **Professional licensing fees** | Oregon LAc license renewal, NCCAOM certification maintenance. |
| **Continuing education** | CEU courses, conferences, related travel — required for license maintenance, fully deductible. |
| **Professional memberships** | POCA, OAAM, ASA, or any other professional association dues. |
| **Business insurance premiums** | All business-related insurance. |
| **Office supplies & software** | Scheduling software, EHR system, payment processing, website hosting. |
| **Marketing** | Website, business cards, flyers, social media advertising. |
| **Furniture & equipment** | Recliners, treatment tables, lamps, sound machines — can be deducted in full under Section 179 or depreciated. |
| **Startup costs** | Up to $5,000 can be deducted in your first year; the rest amortized over 15 years. |
| **Phone & internet** | Business-use percentage of your cell phone and internet bill. |
| **Bank fees & payment processing** | Monthly fees, Square/Stripe transaction fees. |
| **Bookkeeping & tax prep** | CPA fees, accounting software subscription. |

### Vehicle (Business Use of Car)

If Yarrow drives to the clinic, that is a regular commute and is NOT deductible. However, driving between business locations (e.g., Dog Heart PDX client visits, supply runs, going from one business site to another) IS deductible.

Two methods:
1. **Standard mileage rate** — 70 cents/mile for 2025 (verify 2026 rate; typically adjusts slightly). Track every business mile with an app (MileIQ, Everlance, or a simple spreadsheet).
2. **Actual expense method** — Track gas, insurance, repairs, depreciation, and apply business-use percentage.

**Standard mileage is simpler and usually better for most people.** You must choose in the first year you use the vehicle for business.

### Home Office Deduction

Only applies if Yarrow uses a dedicated space in their home *regularly and exclusively* for business. If the acupuncture practice operates out of a rented clinic, the home office deduction would only apply to admin work done at home (and only if that space is used exclusively for that purpose).

- **Simplified method:** $5/sq ft, up to 300 sq ft = max $1,500 deduction
- **Regular method:** Calculate actual percentage of home used, apply to rent/mortgage interest, utilities, insurance, etc.

**Likely more relevant for Dog Heart PDX** if Yarrow does admin, scheduling, and client communication from a home office.

### Health Insurance Premium Deduction (Above-the-Line)

Self-employed individuals can deduct 100% of health insurance premiums (medical, dental, vision) for themselves and dependents as an above-the-line deduction on Form 1040 (not on Schedule C — it's even better because it reduces AGI). This applies when:
- You are not eligible for an employer-subsidized plan (note: Yarrow's part-time cafe work may or may not offer this)
- The plan is in your name or your business's name
- Deduction cannot exceed net SE income

## 5. Managing Two Businesses — Dog Heart PDX + Acupuncture Practice

### Should They Be Under One LLC?

**Recommendation: Two separate LLCs (or one LLC and one sole prop).**

Reasons:
1. **Liability isolation.** If a dog bites a client through Dog Heart PDX, you don't want that claim reaching acupuncture practice assets, and vice versa. Separate entities = separate liability shields.
2. **Clean financials.** Separate bank accounts, separate P&L statements, separate Schedule Cs. Much easier for bookkeeping and tax filing.
3. **Professional credibility.** Insurance companies, landlords, and credentialing bodies expect a healthcare practice to be its own entity.
4. **Different risk profiles.** Pet sitting/dog training and acupuncture have completely different insurance, regulatory, and liability landscapes.

### Tax Implications

- Each business gets its own **Schedule C** on Yarrow's personal tax return (Form 1040).
- Net income from BOTH businesses is combined for self-employment tax purposes.
- Losses from one can offset income from the other (within limits).
- Quarterly estimated payments should account for TOTAL expected self-employment income from both businesses plus any W-2 income from the cafe.
- There is no extra cost to filing two Schedule Cs — it's just more lines on the same return.

### The Dog Heart PDX Question

If Dog Heart PDX is currently operating as a sole proprietorship (no LLC), that is fine for now — it's lower-risk than acupuncture. But if Yarrow wants liability protection there too (recommended if doing in-home pet sitting), forming a second LLC is another $100 + $100/year.

**Minimum viable approach:** Form the LLC for the acupuncture practice. Keep Dog Heart PDX as a sole prop with a DBA if it's small and adequately covered by insurance. Revisit if Dog Heart PDX grows.

## 6. Oregon-Specific Taxes — Which Apply?

Portland-area businesses face a uniquely layered tax environment. Here is what applies and what doesn't at typical community acupuncture income levels.

### Oregon Corporate Activity Tax (CAT)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What** | A gross receipts tax on commercial activity in Oregon |
| **Rate** | $250 + 0.57% of taxable commercial activity above $1 million |
| **Filing threshold** | Must register if Oregon commercial activity exceeds $750,000 |
| **Tax threshold** | Owe tax only if Oregon commercial activity exceeds $1 million |
| **Applies to Yarrow?** | **No.** A community acupuncture practice will be well under $750K. No action needed. |

### Portland Business License Tax

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What** | City income tax on business net income |
| **Rate** | 2.6% of net income apportioned to Portland |
| **Exemption** | Businesses with gross revenue under $100,000 are exempt from PAYING the tax but should still REGISTER |
| **Minimum tax** | None if under exemption threshold |
| **Applies to Yarrow?** | **Probably not in Year 1** if gross revenue stays under $100K. But **register anyway** — it's required. If/when revenue crosses $100K, the tax kicks in. |

### Multnomah County Business Income Tax (MCBIT)

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What** | County income tax on business net income |
| **Rate** | 2.0% of net income apportioned to Multnomah County |
| **Exemption** | Same $100,000 gross revenue threshold |
| **Filed with** | Portland business tax — one combined return |
| **Applies to Yarrow?** | **Same as Portland — register, likely exempt from payment in Year 1.** |

### Metro Supportive Housing Services (SHS) Tax

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What** | Personal income tax for the Metro region (Multnomah, Clackamas, Washington counties) |
| **Rate** | 1% on taxable income above $125,000 (single) / $200,000 (joint) |
| **Business component** | Businesses with gross receipts over $5 million pay 0.75% |
| **Applies to Yarrow?** | **The personal income portion applies only if Yarrow's total taxable income exceeds $125K.** Very unlikely in the first few years. The business gross receipts tax does not apply (way under $5M). **No action needed unless income is high.** |

### Multnomah County Preschool for All (PFA) Tax

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What** | Personal income tax surcharge in Multnomah County |
| **Rate** | 1.5% on taxable income above $125,000 (single) / $200,000 (joint); additional 1.5% above $250,000 (single) / $400,000 (joint) |
| **Business component** | Businesses with gross receipts over $5 million pay 0.8% |
| **Applies to Yarrow?** | **Same as SHS — only if personal taxable income exceeds $125K.** Very unlikely early on. **No action needed unless income is high.** |

### Portland Clean Energy Community Benefits Fund (PCEF) Surcharge

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What** | Surcharge on large retailers |
| **Threshold** | Portland-sourced revenue over $1 billion AND total revenue over $500 million |
| **Rate** | 1% on Portland-sourced revenue |
| **Applies to Yarrow?** | **Absolutely not.** This targets big-box retailers. |

### Summary for Yarrow (Year 1)

| Tax | Action Required? |
|-----|-----------------|
| Oregon CAT | No |
| Portland Business License Tax | Register; likely exempt from payment |
| Multnomah County BIT | Register (same form as Portland); likely exempt |
| Metro SHS | No (income too low) |
| PFA | No (income too low) |
| PCEF | No |

**The big ones that DO apply from Day 1:** Federal income tax, federal SE tax (15.3%), and Oregon income tax. That's where the money goes.

## 7. Bookkeeping Basics

### Recommended Tools

| Tool | Cost | Best For |
|------|------|----------|
| **Wave** | Free | Solo practitioners on a budget. Invoicing, receipts, basic reports. No payroll (payroll is paid add-on). |
| **QuickBooks Self-Employed** | ~$15/month | Better mobile app, mileage tracking built in, integrates with TurboTax. Good if Yarrow wants quarterly tax estimates calculated automatically. |
| **QuickBooks Simple Start** | ~$30/month | If Yarrow plans to grow, hire employees, or wants more robust reporting. Overkill for Year 1. |
| **Hurdlr** | Free tier available | Good for tracking income, expenses, mileage, and estimated taxes in one app. |

**Recommendation for Yarrow:** Start with **Wave** (free) for the acupuncture practice. It handles everything a solo practitioner needs. If it feels clunky, upgrade to QuickBooks Self-Employed.

### What to Track

- **All income** — every patient payment, every session, every product sale. Record date, amount, source, payment method.
- **All expenses** — every business purchase with date, amount, vendor, category, and business purpose.
- **Mileage** — if using personal vehicle for any business driving (supply runs, traveling between business locations). Use an app or a dedicated notebook.
- **Receipts** — photograph or scan every receipt. Wave and QuickBooks both have receipt-scanning features. The IRS requires documentation for all deductions.

### Receipt Retention

- **Federal:** Keep tax records for at least **3 years** from the date you file (or 2 years from the date you paid the tax, whichever is later). Keep records for **6 years** if you underreport income by more than 25%. Keep records **indefinitely** if you don't file or file a fraudulent return.
- **Practical advice:** Keep everything for **7 years.** Digital copies are fine.

### Separating Business and Personal

**This is non-negotiable for LLC protection.** If you commingle funds, a court can "pierce the corporate veil" and your LLC's liability protection becomes worthless.

- [ ] Open a **dedicated business checking account** for the acupuncture practice
- [ ] Get a **business debit card** (or credit card if disciplined)
- [ ] **Never** pay personal expenses from the business account
- [ ] **Never** deposit business income into a personal account
- [ ] Pay yourself via **owner's draw** (transfer from business to personal account) — this is normal and expected for a single-member LLC
- [ ] If Dog Heart PDX is a separate business, it should have its own account too (or at minimum, track its income/expenses completely separately)

## 8. Finding a CPA

### What to Look For

1. **Experience with self-employed individuals and Schedule C filers.** Many CPAs primarily serve W-2 employees or large corporations. You want someone who deals with sole props and single-member LLCs regularly.
2. **Healthcare practice experience** is a plus (understands insurance reimbursements, provider credentialing, healthcare-specific deductions).
3. **Portland-area familiarity** — knows the Portland/Multnomah County business tax landscape (the combined return, etc.).
4. **Willingness to advise on quarterly estimates** — not just annual filing.
5. **Reasonable fees** — for a solo practitioner, expect $300-$800 for annual tax prep (personal + Schedule C). Run from anyone quoting $2,000+ for a simple return.
6. **Year-round availability** — not just during tax season. You want someone you can call in August when you have a question.

### Where to Find One

- **POCA (People's Organization of Community Acupuncture) forum** — Ask other community acupuncture practitioners in Portland who they use. This is probably the single best source.
- **Oregon Society of CPAs** — [orcpa.org](https://www.orcpa.org) has a "Find a CPA" directory. Filter by specialty.
- **Word of mouth** — Ask other small business owners, especially in healthcare or wellness.
- **SCORE Portland** — Free mentoring for small businesses; mentors can often recommend CPAs. [score.org](https://www.score.org)
- **Portland Community College SBDC** — Small Business Development Center offers free consulting and has CPA referral lists.

### When to Engage

**Now — or at least by summer 2026.** Don't wait until tax season. A 30-minute consultation ($0-$150) before you open can save you thousands by structuring things correctly from the start. Many CPAs offer a free initial consultation.

### Questions to Ask a Prospective CPA

1. How many self-employed clients do you serve?
2. Are you familiar with Portland/Multnomah County business taxes?
3. Can you help me set up quarterly estimated payments?
4. What's your fee for annual tax prep for a single-member LLC with one or two Schedule Cs?
5. Are you available for questions outside of tax season?

## 9. Insurance Beyond Malpractice

### Malpractice / Professional Liability Insurance

- Already covered in startup checklist — $300-$600/year for $1M/$3M coverage
- **Providers:** American Acupuncture Council (AAC), OAAM-affiliated carriers, CM&F Group
- **Required** for practice and insurance credentialing

### General Liability Insurance

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **What it covers** | Slip-and-fall in clinic, property damage, advertising injury |
| **Not the same as** | Malpractice (which covers professional errors). You need BOTH. |
| **Cost** | $300-$600/year for a small clinic |
| **Where to get it** | Often bundled with malpractice through AAC or as a BOP (Business Owner's Policy). Also: Hiscox, Hartford, Next Insurance. |
| **Tip** | Your landlord will almost certainly require a Certificate of Insurance (COI) with them listed as additional insured before you sign a lease. Get this lined up before space-hunting. |

### Property / Renter's Insurance for Clinic Space

- If leasing, your landlord's insurance covers the building but NOT your stuff inside.
- **Renter's/tenant insurance** or a **Business Owner's Policy (BOP)** covers your recliners, equipment, supplies, signage.
- Cost: $200-$500/year depending on coverage limits.
- Often bundled with general liability in a BOP for lower cost than buying separately.

### Workers' Compensation Insurance

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| **When required** | Oregon requires workers' comp for ALL employees (even one part-time employee) |
| **Solo practitioner** | NOT required if you have zero employees. You can optionally cover yourself. |
| **When to get it** | The moment you hire anyone — even a part-time front desk person |
| **Cost** | Varies by classification; acupuncture office staff is relatively low-risk. Expect $0.50-$1.50 per $100 of payroll. |
| **Where** | SAIF Corporation (Oregon's state-chartered workers' comp insurer) or private carriers |
| **Penalty for non-compliance** | Severe — Oregon is strict. Fines plus personal liability for injuries. |

### Health Insurance for Yarrow (Self-Employed)

Yarrow needs personal health insurance. Options:

1. **Oregon Health Insurance Marketplace** — [healthcare.oregon.gov](https://healthcare.oregon.gov)
   - Open enrollment typically November-January for coverage starting January 1
   - Special enrollment period available for qualifying life events
   - **Premium tax credits (subsidies)** are available based on income. As a lower-income self-employed person in Year 1, Yarrow may qualify for significant subsidies.
   - Premiums are deductible as the self-employed health insurance deduction (see Section 4)

2. **Oregon Health Plan (Medicaid)** — If Yarrow's income is low enough (138% of federal poverty level, ~$20,800 for a single person in 2025), they may qualify for OHP, which is free.
   - **Important:** As self-employment income grows, Yarrow may transition off OHP to marketplace plans. Plan for this transition.

3. **Cafe employer plan** — If the cafe offers health insurance to part-time employees, this may be the simplest option. However, if they're on an employer plan, they cannot take the self-employed health insurance deduction.

4. **POCA Co-op health plans** — Check if POCA offers any group purchasing options for members.

### Insurance Summary — Priority Order

| Priority | Insurance | When |
|----------|-----------|------|
| 1 | Malpractice | Before first patient |
| 2 | General liability | Before signing lease |
| 3 | Property/BOP | Before opening |
| 4 | Health insurance | ASAP (open enrollment or life event) |
| 5 | Workers' comp | When hiring first employee |
