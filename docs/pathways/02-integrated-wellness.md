- Anthropology training = genuinely holistic perspective, not just marketing copy
- Multiple modalities already in hand: acupuncture, gua sha, cupping, moxa, yoga
- This pathway builds naturally *on top of* Pathway 1 over time

## The Anthropology Angle
Yarrow's M.S. in Applied Anthropology is a genuine competitive advantage:
- Trained to see the whole person in cultural context — that IS holistic health
- Qualitative research skills = exceptional patient intake and assessment
- Understanding of how social structures, culture, and environment shape health outcomes
- Fieldwork experience with Indigenous communities (Nuwuvi) = deep cultural humility, not performative
- This positions Yarrow as more than "another acupuncturist" — it's a distinct clinical identity

## Recommended Niche (Based on Yarrow's Answers)

> **Trauma-informed integrative wellness**
> Acupuncture + yoga + workshops
> Centering: hormone health, chronic pain, trauma recovery
> Serving: queer & trans community, multilingual patients (EN/FR/ES)
> Rooted in: cultural responsiveness, accessibility, community resilience

### Why This Niche Works in Portland
- Large queer/trans community actively seeking affirming healthcare providers
- Growing demand for trauma-informed everything
- Hormone health is a major search term / patient need
- Few practitioners combine acupuncture + yoga + anthropological perspective
- French-speaking wellness practitioner is genuinely rare

## Service Stack (Phased Rollout)

### Phase 1 (Launch — builds on Pathway 1)
- Community acupuncture sessions (primary income)
- Individual acupuncture for niche populations

### Phase 2 (6–12 months post-launch)
- Trauma-informed yoga classes (small group)
- Wellness workshops (stress, hormone health, seasonal self-care)

### Phase 3 (Year 2+)

## Revenue Streams (Stacked)
1. **Acupuncture sessions** — primary (see Pathway 1 economics)
2. **Yoga classes** — $15–25/person, 6–12 students = $90–300/class
3. **Workshops** — $30–75/person, 8–20 attendees = $240–1,500/workshop
4. **Pop-up events** — community building + income + visibility
5. **Future:** online content, speaking, teaching at colleges

## Business Model Options

### Shared Practice / Collective
- Join or form a wellness collective with complementary practitioners

### Solo Practice with Referral Network
- Own practice with cross-referral relationships
- Partner with naturopaths, therapists, massage therapists, midwives
- Lower overhead, full control
