# Pathway 5: Anthropology-Adjacent Employment

## Overview
Leverage the M.S. in Applied Anthropology from PSU for employment in fields that value qualitative research, cultural competency, and human-centered analysis. This is the "use the degree" pathway.

## Why This Is Worth Exploring
- An M.S. in Applied Anthropology is more marketable than people think — but you have to translate the skills
- Portland has a decent job market for these roles (tech, nonprofits, healthcare systems, government)
- Provides W-2 stability, benefits, and retirement contributions
- Could be combined with part-time acupuncture or dog training on the side

## Career Tracks

### A. UX Research
**Fit:** High — qualitative research IS UX research with different subjects
**Portland market:** Moderate — fewer big tech offices than Seattle, but remote roles are abundant
**Salary:** $70–95K entry, $95–130K mid-level
**Path in:**
- Highlight ethnographic methods, interviewing, thematic analysis on resume
- Build a small UX portfolio (2–3 case studies, can be speculative/academic)
- Take a short course or bootcamp to learn UX-specific vocabulary (Interaction Design Foundation, Coursera — many are free/cheap)
- Apply to: tech companies (remote), design agencies, product consultancies
- Portland-specific: Instrument, Webfor, Metal Toad, Simple (Banno), Act-On, Vacasa

- [ ] Research free/low-cost UX research courses

### B. Nonprofit Program Management
**Fit:** High — anthropologists excel at understanding communities and designing culturally responsive programs
**Portland market:** Strong — Portland has a dense nonprofit sector
**Salary:** $45–65K program coordinator, $55–80K program manager
**Path in:**
- Apply directly — M.S. + service experience is sufficient for many roles
- Emphasize community engagement, cross-cultural competency, program evaluation skills
- Look at: social services, public health, immigrant/refugee services, housing, education

**Portland orgs to research:**
- IRCO (Immigrant & Refugee Community Organization)
- Latino Network
- Native American Youth and Family Center (NAYA)
- Outside In
- Central City Concern
- Portland Parks & Recreation (community programs)
- Meyer Memorial Trust (grantmaking)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Search current openings at Portland nonprofits (Idealist, Mac's List, Oregon Nonprofit Association)
- [ ] Draft nonprofit-focused resume version
- [ ] Identify 5–10 orgs aligned with Yarree's interests

### C. Public Health
**Fit:** Strong — medical anthropology overlaps heavily with public health practice
**Portland market:** Good — Multnomah County, Oregon Health Authority, healthcare systems
**Salary:** $50–75K entry-level, more with experience
**Path in:**
- Community health worker, health educator, program evaluator roles
- Acupuncture training is a bonus — shows commitment to health + wellness modalities
- Government roles often have clear pay scales, benefits, retirement (PERS)

**Agencies to research:**
- Multnomah County Health Department
- Oregon Health Authority (OHA)
- Kaiser Permanente (community health)
- Providence (community benefit programs)
- OHSU (research or community roles)

**Action items:**
- [ ] Search governmentjobs.com and Oregon state job board for relevant roles
- [ ] Draft public health-focused resume version
- [ ] Look into Community Health Worker certification (Oregon has a formal pathway)

### D. Market Research / Consulting
**Fit:** Moderate-high — qualitative research skills are valuable in commercial settings
**Portland market:** Moderate
**Salary:** $55–80K research analyst, $70–100K+ senior/consultant
**Path in:**
- Market research firms, brand consultancies, design thinking agencies
- Position as: qualitative researcher, consumer insights analyst, research consultant
- Emphasize: interviewing methodology, coding/analysis, synthesis, presentation

**Portland firms:**
- Davis Hibbitts & Midghall (DHM Research)
- Kelton Global
- Various ad/branding agencies with research arms

### E. Museum / Cultural Institutions
**Fit:** Direct — this is what anthropology degrees are "supposed" to lead to
**Portland market:** Limited but real
**Salary:** $40–55K (notoriously underpaid sector)
**Path in:**
- Curatorial, education, collections, community engagement roles
- Portland has fewer options but they exist

**Institutions:**
- Portland Art Museum (PAM)
- Oregon Museum of Science and Industry (OMSI)
- Oregon Historical Society
- Portland Chinatown Museum
- Tribal cultural organizations (Confederated Tribes of Grand Ronde, Warm Springs)

### F. Education / Academic Support
**Fit:** Moderate — if Yarree enjoyed the academic environment
**Salary:** $45–65K
**Options:**
- PSU advising, student services, or research coordination (alumni advantage)
- PCC or other community colleges — adjunct teaching anthropology
- Research assistant on funded projects
- Grant writing (anthropologists are trained writers)

## Skills Translation Guide

| Anthropology Skill | Professional Translation |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| Ethnographic fieldwork | Qualitative user research, stakeholder interviews |
| Participant observation | Contextual inquiry, field studies |
| Thematic coding & analysis | Data analysis, pattern recognition, insight synthesis |
| Writing ethnographies | Report writing, executive summaries, white papers |
| Cross-cultural competency | DEI knowledge, inclusive design, cultural humility |
| Literature review | Competitive analysis, landscape assessment |
| IRB / research ethics | Research governance, ethical data practices |
| Thesis defense | Executive presentations, stakeholder communication |

## Resume Strategy for This Pathway
The key challenge: translating academic language into professional language without losing substance. The master resume should have a "skills translation" section that maps anthropology training to job requirements.

## Key Questions for Yarree
- Did you enjoy the research/academic parts of the M.S., or was it more of a slog?
- Is there a specific sector (health, tech, nonprofits, culture) that calls to you?
- How important are benefits and stability vs. autonomy and flexibility?
- Would you consider remote work, or strongly prefer in-person in Portland?
- Did you build any professional connections through PSU that could be leveraged?
- What was your thesis/capstone about? (This often points toward the best-fit sector)
