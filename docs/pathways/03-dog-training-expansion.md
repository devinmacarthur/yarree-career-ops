# Pathway 3: Dog Training Business Expansion

## Overview
Take the existing dog training / pet sitting business from side-income to primary income by scaling services, raising prices, and/or adding higher-value offerings.

## Current State
- Business exists and has steady (if minimal) clients
- Revenue primarily from house/pet sitting, not training
- Training services are offered but underutilized
- Yarree already handles all business operations

## The Problem to Solve
Pet sitting is labor-intensive and low-margin — you trade hours for dollars at someone else's home. Dog training has much better economics per hour. The goal is to shift the revenue mix toward training while keeping sitting as supplemental income.

## Revenue Comparison (Portland Market)

| Service | Typical Rate | Hours/Client | $/Hour Effective |
|---------|-------------|-------------|-----------------|
| Pet sitting (overnight) | $50–80/night | 12–16 hrs present | $4–6/hr |
| Drop-in pet visits | $20–35/visit | 0.5–1 hr | $20–35/hr |
| Private dog training session | $80–150/session | 1–1.5 hrs | $55–100/hr |
| Group training class (6-week) | $150–250/dog, 6–10 dogs | 1.5 hrs/week × 6 | $100–250/hr |
| Board-and-train (2 weeks) | $1,500–3,000 | Variable | High total, labor-intensive |
| Behavior consultation | $150–300/session | 1.5–2 hrs | $75–150/hr |

## Growth Strategies

### A. Shift to Training-First
- Rebrand/remarket the business as training-primary
- Raise training prices if currently below market
- Offer pet sitting only to training clients as a value-add
- Goal: 8–12 private training clients/week = $640–1,800/week

### B. Add Group Classes
- Rent space at a community center, dog-friendly brewery, or park
- 6-week courses: puppy basics, leash manners, recall
- 6–10 dogs per class × $175 = $1,050–1,750 per class cycle
- Run 2–3 classes simultaneously = $2,100–5,250 per 6-week cycle
- Low overhead, high margin

### C. Behavior Specialization
- Behavior work (reactivity, anxiety, aggression) commands premium rates
- Fewer practitioners, higher demand
- Yarree's anthropology background (understanding behavior in context) is directly applicable
- May want additional certification (CPDT-KA, IAABC) to charge top rates

### D. Online / Hybrid Training
- Virtual training sessions for basic obedience and follow-up
- Pre-recorded courses (passive income)
- Lower barrier for clients, expands geographic reach

### E. Partner with Vets and Shelters
- Referral relationships with Portland veterinary clinics
- Contract work with shelters (behavior assessment, training programs)
- Oregon Humane Society, Multnomah County Animal Services, smaller rescues
- Provides steady baseline income + visibility

## Scaling Path (12-Month Plan)

| Month | Focus | Target Revenue |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 1–2 | Rebrand, update pricing, marketing push | $1,500–2,000/mo |
| 3–4 | Launch first group class series | $2,500–3,500/mo |
| 5–6 | Add second class + raise private rates | $3,500–4,500/mo |
| 7–9 | Build vet/shelter referral pipeline | $4,000–5,500/mo |
| 10–12 | Consider behavior specialization, online offerings | $5,000–7,000/mo |

## Startup Costs (Minimal)
- Updated website/branding: $200–500 (DIY) or $1,000–2,000 (designer)
- Group class space rental: $50–150/session
- Marketing (social media ads, flyers at vet offices): $100–300/mo
- Certification (CPDT-KA exam): $385 + study materials
- Total: $500–3,000 depending on approach

## Yarree's Advantages
- Already has a business and client base (even if small)
- Dog handling experience from sitting work
- Understanding of animal behavior + human behavior (anthropology + dog training overlap is real)
- Portland is an extremely dog-friendly market
