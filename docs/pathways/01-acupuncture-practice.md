- Yarrow's niche populations (queer/trans, trauma) are underserved and growing in Portland

## The Recommended Model: Community Acupuncture (POCA)

**Why community over private:**
- Yarrow already trained in this model for 2 years
- Aligns with their stated motivation: "accessible holistic healthcare and community resilience"
- "Both sound good" on 1:1 vs community — but all their experience is community
- High volume = faster income ramp to hit $3,333/mo target
- POCA provides cooperative support, business resources, and community

**Economics:**
- Sliding scale: $20–50/treatment
- Treat 4–8 patients/hour in group room
- At capacity (25–30 hrs/week): $60,000–100,000/year
- Yarrow's $3,333/mo target = achievable at ~15–20 hrs/week patient time

**Startup costs (POCA model):**
- Recliners (4–8): $200–800 (used)
- Treatment supplies: $500–1,000
- Rent (shared or small space): $800–1,500/mo
- Malpractice insurance: $300–600/year
- Business license, signage: $300–500
- Total startup: $3,000–6,000
